This video shows how a social network can become weaker when certain key people are removed.

We use a Facebook friendship network and remove one important person at a time. After each removal, we measure how well the network still holds together. The graph at the bottom shows that strength going down over time.

Content in the Video
    1. The network appears on screen.
    2. One person is highlighted (this is the next target).
    3. That person is removed from the network.
    4. A score called λ_2  updates to show the network's new strength.
    5. This repeats for several removals.

Simple Meaning of λ_2
    - Higher λ_2: the network is strongly connected.
    - Lower λ_2: the network is easier to break apart.
    - By decreasing λ_2  (Fiedler value), it becomes harder to traverse from one point of the network to another.
        ○ A higher Fiedler value indicates a more robust and well-connected graph
        ○ A lower value suggests that the graph is more fragile and may be easily disconnected by removing a few nodes or edges

Why This Matters
This is a simple way to show that some people are much more important than others for keeping a community connected. If those key people leave, information and influence spread less effectively.

Targeted removal of a few structurally important nodes can noticeably weaken an otherwise dense social network. This concept of strength in networks is relevant to several fields such as biology (in gene co-expression networks) and computer science (in google page rank and clustering)
